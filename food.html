About Me

Teri grew up in the Midwest, has lived on both coasts of the U.S. and has traveled extensively across the globe, which she attributes to her early curiosity and later passion for the many cuisines she’s encountered. Before earning her degree in culinary arts from a Le Cordon Bleu school in Portland, OR she spent a decade working in the footwear industry for both Adidas and Nike. Since coming into the culinary world she has owned and operated a ghost kitchen in the Pacific Northwest and later earned her degree as a holistic health coach. She furthered her studies as a licensed digestive health specialist, has co-executed an online health coaching and cooking program and most recently has done private chef work in the Seacoast area of New Hampshire.

Mutton Biryani

We think this is a truly amazing and delicious version of the classic Indian layered rice dish. Our method is close to the traditional way, except we finish ours in the oven rather than on the stovetop. This ensures even heating, which acts as an insurance policy against burning. Save the leftover onion oil – it's very fragrant and flavorful and can be used in other sauces.
